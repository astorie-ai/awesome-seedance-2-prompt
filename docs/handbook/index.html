Variants at a glance

VariantBest forTrade-off
Seedance 2 ProHero shots, final renders, client deliverablesSlowest, highest cost per second
Seedance 2 LiteIteration, A/B prompt testing, bulk variationsSlightly softer motion at edges
Seedance 2 i2vImage-to-video — animate an existing stillQuality bounded by the source image

What Seedance 2 is good at

  • Physics — fabric, liquid, hair, and contact behave plausibly
  • Camera control — dolly, push-in, orbit, parallax, crane reliably follow the prompt
  • Subject identity — faces, products, and characters hold across the clip
  • Lighting consistency — exposure and color stay stable through a shot

Production workflow

The workflow we recommend on the Martini Art canvas:

  1. Draft your prompt using a recipe from Awesome Seedance 2 Prompts
  2. Run a quick Seedance 2 Lite batch (4–8 variants) to lock the motion direction
  3. Optionally compare against Veo 3.1 or Kling 3.0 on the same prompt — the canvas runs them in parallel
  4. Promote the winning seed to Seedance 2 Pro for the final render
  5. Stitch multi-shot scenes on the canvas timeline with Soul V2 for character continuity

FAQ

Which Seedance 2 variant should I use?

Pro for finals, Lite for iteration, i2v when you have a source image. See the table above for the full trade-off.

Does Seedance 2 support image-to-video?

Yes. The i2v variant keeps subject identity stable while applying the motion and camera moves you describe in the prompt. Recipes are in the prompts library.

Where do I get the API?

ByteDance ships Seedance via their official API. Martini Art wraps it alongside Veo 3.1, Kling 3.0, Soul V2, and Nano Banana 2 so you can compare and stitch without writing API code.
